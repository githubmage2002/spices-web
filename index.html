Cinnamon

True cinnamon (also known as Ceylon cinnamon) is the most desirable variety of this spice—and it originates in Sri Lanka. Ceylon cinnamon is renowned for its unique flavour, colour, and aroma.

Curry Leaves

Curry leaves (known as “Karapincha”) are a staple in Sri Lanka gardens and cooking. The leaves possess a strong fragrance, making them an ideal seasoning.

Cardamom

Cardamom is one of the world’s most valued spices and the most expensive in Sri Lanka. Its value has earned it the nickname: The Queen of Spices.

Buy now
Turmeric

Turmeric is an iconic spice throughout Asian countries, particularly in Sri Lanka. It’s what gives Sri Lankan curries their distinct golden-yellow colour.

Buy now
Cloves

Cloves originated in Indonesia. However, they are grown and harvested throughout Southeast Asia, Africa, and Sri Lanka. These dried flower buds have an intense aroma and warm, spicy flavour.

Ginger

Ginger is a heavily used spice in Sri Lanka. People choose it for its sharp flavour, subtle heat, and mild sweetness, which gives curries a unique and complex taste.
